Regressions in Covariances, Dependencies and Graphs

Multivariate data routinely collected nowadays using modern technological devices display both cross-sectional and temporal dependence. This book emphasizes the phenomenal role of the idea of regression in dealing with dependencies via modeling covariances, copulas and graphs using the twin principles of parsimony for classical data and regularization for modern high-dimensional data. Parsimonious parametric (linear, quadratic,...) and non- parametric models using covariates for covariance and precision matrices, distributions and graphs are presented. Regularization methods using Lasso penalty and the like for sparse estimation of (un-)directed graphical models, time series and spatial models are covered. Special emphasis is placed on the central role of the class of graphical Lasso algorithms in the modern high-dimensional data analysis. The algorithms and methodologies are illustrated using genuine datasets. At the end of each chapter, practical, ready-to-run R scripts bring every concept to life, re- inforcing both understanding and hands-on application. A companion R package recode is specifically designed to complement the book’s content, featuring curated real-world and simulated datasets along with a variety of functions to implement and visualize the covered concepts. Whether you’re a student, researcher or data scientist, the book, together with its accompanying R package and practical R scripts, bridge the gap between theory and practice, providing the tools you need to apply advanced and some state-of-the-art statistical methods to real-world scenarios.
